Your Pest Solution Experts: Detailed Termite Control Services

At Burlington Termite, our termite control services are designed to be thorough, effective, and tailored to the specific needs of your property. We utilize a multi-faceted approach to ensure complete eradication and long-term prevention.

Comprehensive Termite Inspection

The first step in effective termite control is a detailed inspection. Our trained technicians conduct a thorough assessment of your property, both interior and exterior, to identify signs of termite activity, locate colony locations, and determine the extent of the infestation. This includes:

Interior Inspection

During the interior inspection, we examine areas prone to termite activity, such as basements, crawl spaces, attics, and areas around plumbing. We look for mud tubes, damaged wood, discarded wings, and other indicators of a termite presence. Special attention is given to areas with potential moisture issues or structural vulnerabilities that might attract termites.

Exterior Inspection

The exterior inspection focuses on identifying entry points and signs of termite activity around the foundation, wood structures in the yard, and areas where wood makes contact with the soil. We check for mud tubes on the foundation walls, damaged fence posts, and other exterior indicators. This comprehensive evaluation helps us understand how termites are accessing your property and the scale of the infestation.

Customized Treatment Plans

Based on the findings of our inspection, we develop a customized treatment plan tailored to your specific situation. We employ a range of advanced treatment methods depending on the type of termites found, the severity of the infestation, and your property's unique characteristics. Our goal is to recommend the most effective and least disruptive solution.

Trenching and Rodding

For subterranean termites, which are common in the South Burlington area, trenching and rodding involves creating a treated zone around the perimeter of your property's foundation. A liquid termiticide is applied to the soil, creating a barrier that eliminates termites attempting to enter or exit the structure. This method is highly effective in preventing future infestations.

Direct Wood Treatment

In cases where termites have already infested structural wood, we employ direct wood treatment. This involves injecting or applying termiticides directly into the affected wood. This method targets termites within the wood and provides localized protection. This approach is often used in conjunction with other treatment methods for comprehensive control.

Baiting Systems

Termite baiting systems offer a non-disruptive approach to colony elimination. Bait stations are installed around the perimeter of your property. Termites discover the bait, consume it, and share it with other members of the colony, leading to a gradual decline and eventual elimination of the entire colony. This method is effective for long-term control and monitoring.

Our technicians will explain the recommended treatment approach in detail, answering any questions you may have and ensuring you understand the process and expected outcomes. We prioritize transparency and want you to feel confident in the solutions we provide.

Preventative Treatments

Your Pest Solution Experts: Common Termite Problems and Signs You Need Our Service

Recognizing the signs of a termite infestation early can save you significant time, money, and stress. Termites are often referred to as 'silent destroyers' because they can cause extensive damage discreetly before the signs become obvious. Here are some common problems and indicators that you might have a termite issue and need the professional help of Burlington Termite:

Mud Tubes on Foundation Walls

Subterranean termites build pencil-sized mud tubes from soil and wood to travel between their underground nests and their food source (your home's wooden structures). Finding these tubes on your foundation walls, in crawl spaces, or on exterior walls is a strong indicator of a termite infestation. These tubes provide a protected pathway for termites, shielding them from predators and drying out. If you see these, it's crucial to have your property inspected immediately to determine the extent of the infestation and begin treatment.

These mud tubes are a clear visual cue. Don't ignore them! They represent active movement of termites and mean your property is at risk. While it might be tempting to knock them down, it's better to leave them intact for our technicians to assess during their inspection. Disrupting them might make it harder to locate the source of the infestation. Our experts know exactly what to look for and how to interpret these signs.

Damaged or Hollow-Sounding Wood

Termites consume wood from the inside out, often leaving the exterior shell intact. If you tap on wooden surfaces in your home and they sound hollow or if you notice blistering or darkening of the wood, it could be a sign of termite damage. This damage can progress rapidly, compromising the structural integrity of your property if left untreated. Areas particularly vulnerable include door frames, window sills, and wooden flooring.

This internal damage is why termites are so insidious. They can be working away at your home's foundation or support beams without you even realizing it. By the time the damage is visible, it can be extensive. This is why professional inspections are so important – we can detect these internal signs long before they become obvious to the untrained eye. Catching the problem early is key to minimizing damage and repair costs.

Sagging Floors or Ceilings

Severe termite infestations can weaken wooden support structures, leading to sagging floors or ceilings. This is a clear indication of advanced termite damage and requires immediate attention from a professional termite control service. Sagging is a serious structural issue caused by the loss of strength in the wood due to termite activity. Addressing this requires not only termite eradication but also potential structural repairs.

If you observe any unusal sagging or instability in your floors or ceilings, don't delay. This is a symptom of a significant structural problem likely caused by termites. The longer you wait, the more extensive and costly the repairs will be. Our team can assess the damage and develop a comprehensive plan that addresses both the active infestation and the necessary structural remediation.

Discarded Termite Wings

Swarming termites, which are reproductive termites that leave the colony to establish new ones, shed their wings after flying. You might find piles of discarded wings around windowsills, doorways, or other light sources. This is a sign that a termite swarm has occurred near or inside your property, indicating a nearby termite colony. These discarded wings resemble tiny fish scales and are a clear warning sign that termites are active in your vicinity.

Seeing these discarded wings suggests that a reproductive swarm has taken place. This means there's likely a mature termite colony nearby, and there's a high probability of an active infestation in or around your property. Don't mistake these wings for other insect debris. If you find these, it's time to call the experts. We can identify the species of termite and locate the source of the swarm.

Termite Droppings (Frass)

Drywood termites, another type of termite found in some areas, expel wood-colored fecal pellets called frass. You might find small piles of these pellets near infested wood. The appearance of frass is a strong indicator of a drywood termite infestation. These droppings are often found in small mounds near tiny holes in the wood, which are kick-out holes used by the termites to push the frass out of their tunnels.

The presence of frass is a definite sign unique to drywood termites. Unlike subterranean termites, drywood termites don't need contact with the soil. They live entirely within the wood they infest. If you notice what appears to be tiny piles of sawdust or sand near wooden furniture, beams, or trim, it's essential to investigate further. Our technicians are trained to identify frass and distinguish it from other types of debris.

Bubbling or Peeling Paint

Termite activity behind walls can cause bubbling or peeling paint due to moisture and damage to the underlying wood. If you notice unexplained bubbling or peeling paint on interior or exterior walls, especially near the baseboards or trim, it could be a sign of hidden termite damage. This is often a later sign of infestation, indicating that the termites have been active in that area for some time.

This type of paint damage is a consequence of the termites altering the moisture content and structure of the wood beneath. It's a less obvious sign compared to mud tubes or visible damage, but it's still a crucial indicator. If you see mysterious bubbling or peeling paint, investigate further and consider a professional termite inspection. It's always better to be safe than sorry when it comes to potential termite damage.

Clicking or Rustling Sounds in Walls

While less common, in severe infestations, you might hear faint clicking or rustling sounds coming from inside your walls. This sound is created by termites chewing through wood. If you hear these sounds, it's a strong indication of a significant termite presence within your walls. These sounds are most often audible in quiet environments and can be a disturbing sign of active infestation within your home's structure.

Hearing sounds within your walls is understandably alarming. While other pests might cause sounds, clicking or rustling associated with wood consumption is often attributed to termites. If you suspect you hear termite activity, it's important to have a professional investigation. Our team can use specialized equipment, like acoustic detectors, to confirm the presence of termites and pinpoint their location within your walls.
